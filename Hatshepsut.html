THE WOMAN WHO WANTED THE POWER OF EGYPT

The female pharaoh Hatshepsut, that ruled during the New Kingdom, was the first pharaoh to ever be a female. In Egypt pharaohs were supposed to be regarded mostly like men, since this was such a problem she decided to act like a man, wearing a fake beard at all times to be shown the respect of a pharaoh. Her pride is what made her one of the greatest pharaohs of all time, and her achievements can back that.

HER STORY

It is estimated that Hatshepsut was born in 1504 B.C.E, while other historians will say otherwise. She was the older daughter of the 18th dynasty king, Thutmose I. Thutmose I had two wives Mutnofret and Iset or otherwise known as Isis. When Thutmose I died, the person to take on the throne would be his son. Since most of Mutnofret’s sons had died prematurely, Thutmose II inherited throne and Hatshepsut was his wife. Hatshepsut only had one child, Neferure, and never had a son. Thutmose II died at a young age and when he did the throne inherited by his only son, Thutmose III who was the son of the other wife of Thutmose II, Isis. Since he was so young he had to have a regent or
someone to help him rule, and that person was Hatshepsut. Hatshepsut was Thutmose III’s regent for around 7 years until she had full royal titulary, in other words she was the co-ruler of Egypt. Hatshepsut had much more rule over Thutmose III because of their age difference. Since she still looked like a queen, she changed her look to a more masculine making her look more conflicting to the citizens of Egypt. Even in portraits she looked much like a man, making her look like a better king than ever before. Towards the end of rule she gave a Thutmose III a more meaningful role in state affairs. Even though she did so much to benefit Egypt her efforts were wasted, during Thutmose III rule, he ordered to destroy all of the monuments Hatshepsut made and all of the monuments she restored.

THE PHAROAH'S ACCOMPLISHMENTS

Hatshepsut wasn’t just a pharaoh that was a woman, she had many other accomplishments, including trade, restoration, and the construction of many monuments. First of all, she had a very peaceful reign, there was no war, she focused mostly on the trade of Egypt. All though all of this is true, in the start of her rule she had a successful military campaign in Nubia. Hatshepsut also had a very succesful seaborne trading expeditionto Punt which was on the southernmost end of the Red Sea. They traded animal skins, ebony, gold, processed myrrh, myrrh trees, and baboons. She also had the royal duty of restoring and building, she took a very excessive building process. In the Thebes she made temples focused on her divine father, Amon -Re. At the Karnak temple she remodeled her father hypostle hall, made the Red Chapel, and made to pairs of obelisks. Her greatest achievement by far was the Dayr al-Baḥrī temple. It was funerary monument, for when she passed away. The temple was also dedicated to Amon-Re, Osiris, Anubis, Re, Hathor, Anubis, and the royal ancestors. They all had a dedicated chapel, she extended her father’s tomb so that she could rest with her father. All in all, Hatshepsut improved Egypt’s trade, restored monuments, and made Egypt peaceful.
